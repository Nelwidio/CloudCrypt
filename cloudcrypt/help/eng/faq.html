FAQ

What is CloudCrypt?

CloudCrypt is a software, which allows a user to save his personal data in a cloud, after automatically encrypted.

What is the license CloudCrypt is provided under?

CloudCrypt is provided under the GNU Lesser General Public License Version 3 (LGPLv3).

Why do you use a open-source license?

The students creating CloudCrypt are supporters of the open-source movement. It is important for us, that a user of a software is able to have the absolute control over his software. This includes an open source-code and the right to improve the software or to modify it according to one's needs. A common misconception is the thinking, that free software must always be free of charge. According to Dr. Richard Stallman (one of the founders of the open-source movement) free in free software refers to free in freedom, not in free beer, although we agreed not to charge any money for CloudCrypt.

Which encryption is used?

All data, you store in the cloud with CloudCrypt is encrypted with the Advanced Encryption Standard (AES) with a keylength of 256 bits. We are not using an own implementation of AES, of course. Instead we use the GNU Pricacy Guard.

Does CloudCrypt have any requirements?

In order to use CloudCrypt properly, you have to meet the following requirements:

  • a standard PC or notebook
  • Microsoft Windows 7 or higher or a distribution of Linux
  • Python Version 3.x
  • PyQt5
  • GNU Privacy Guard
  • a valid account of Dropbox

Which are the cloud-services supported by CloudCrypt?

At the moment we support Dropbox only.
